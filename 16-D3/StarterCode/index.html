Correlations Discovered Between Age, Income, Poverty Level Rate & Health Risks

Observation 1: Percentage of Population in Poverty. In general, health concerns tend to increase with increasing poverty percentage within a US state. Additionally, there tends to be higher poverty in Southeastern States, along with higher incidences of all three comparisons provided.

Observation 2: Median Age. Rather than an US trend, age comparisons by particular US States seem to be more insightful. For example, WV has a higher age median, better healthcare, but higher percentages of obesity ad smokers. Likewise, Northeastern States tend to have a higher age median and better healthcare.

Observation 3: Median Household Income. There appears to be a link between household income and the occurrence of obesity and smoking: The higher the income the less likelihood the two idicators occur together. Comparing Household Income vs a Lack of Healthcare, reveals a strong link between health risks and those with lesser incomes. Further, Southeastern States seem to rank lower compared to Northeastern states. Interestingly, TX and MA seem to have a high percentage lacking healthcare, while the extreme opposite is true for MA.
